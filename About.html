Bitcoin: The Future of Finance

Introduction

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that operates on a peer-to-peer network without a central authority. It was introduced in 2008 by an anonymous entity known as Satoshi Nakamoto.

Features

  • Decentralization: No central authority or single point of failure.
  • Security: Transactions are secured through cryptographic techniques.
  • Transparency: All transactions are recorded on a public ledger called the blockchain.
  • Limited Supply: The total supply of Bitcoin is capped at 21 million.

History

Bitcoin was conceptualized in a 2008 whitepaper by Satoshi Nakamoto and launched in January 2009. Over the years, Bitcoin has gained significant attention as a form of digital gold and an alternative to traditional currencies.
